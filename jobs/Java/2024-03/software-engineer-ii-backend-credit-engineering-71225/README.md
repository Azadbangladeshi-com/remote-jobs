Affirm is reinventing credit to make it more honest and friendly, giving consumers the flexibility to buy now and pay later without any hidden fees or compounding interest.

Join the Credit Engineering team and partner with Product and Credit Risk to solve complex challenges on how to protect consumers from overextending themselves while enabling Affirm to be where consumers need us most. As part of the team, you will focus on designing and implementing new features in our underwriting platform to support our partnered merchants and our users across a wide number of interfaces, including Affirm Card, ecommerce sites, and the app. You will have a direct impact on the performance of Affirm as we look to continue to grow and expand the reach out of Affirm loans in a safe and responsible way for our customers!

### What You'll Do

  * Design and build new features for our credit underwriting platform
  * Work with product managers and credit risk analysts to influence the strategy and delivery of the underwriting platform and its capabilities
  * Work with technical leaders in the team to design and implement insightful, forward-looking approaches that go beyond the direct team and solve large open-ended problems
  * Actively contribute to components of our underwriting system while also improving the health of surrounding systems
  * Collaborate with engineers within our team and across the organization to build the next generation of the financial products
  * Learn and improve engineering standards, tooling, and processes across the platform

### What We Look For

  * 1-3 years of software engineering experience
  * Eagerness to learn new things and the curiosity to understand the unknown
  * Familiarity with Python, AWS, Java/Kotlin, MySQL, Github is a plus
  * Strong communication and collaboration skills
  * Empathy to help support our partners and teammates
  * Prior experience building, launching and maintaining consumer scale products
  * Strong cross-team communication and collaboration skills
  * Experience scaling systems in a service oriented architecture preferred but not required
